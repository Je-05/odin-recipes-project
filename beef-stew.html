Beef Stew:

Beef stew is a hearty, comforting dish made with tender chunks of beef, slow-simmered with carrots, potatoes, and other vegetables in a rich, flavorful broth. The stew develops deep flavors from herbs, garlic, and red wine, creating a warm and filling meal perfect for cold days. It's thickened as it cooks, resulting in a savory, satisfying one-pot dish often served with crusty bread or over mashed potatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs (900g) Beef Chuck, Cut Into 1-inch Cubes
  • 3 Tablespoons All-Purpose Flour
  • Salt and Pepper to Taste
  • 2 Tablespoons Olive Oil
  • 1 Large Onion, Chopped
  • 3 Garlic Cloves, Minced
  • 4 Carrots, Peeled and Cut Into Chunks
  • 3 Potatoes, Peeled and Cut Into Chunks
  • 2 Celery Stalks, Chopped
  • 3 Cups Beef Broth
  • 1 Cup Red Wine, (Optional, or Use More Beef Broth)
  • 2 Tablespoons Tomato Paste
  • 1 Tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 Bay Leaves
  • 1 Teaspoon Dired Thyme
  • 1 Teaspoon Dired Rosemary
  • 1 Cup Frozen Peas, (Optional, added at the end
  • Fresh Parsley, Chopped (Optional, for Garnish)

Cooking Steps:

  1. In a Bowl, toss the beef cubes with salt, pepper, and 2 tablespoons of flour until the beef is evenly coated.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Brown the beef in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pot. Cook for about 3-4 minutes per side until the beef is golden brown. Transfer the browned beef to a plate and set aside.
  3. In the Same Pot, add another tablespoon of olive oil. Saut√© the chopped onions and celery for about 5 minutes, until softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes to caramelize. Then, pour in the red wine (if using) to deglaze the pot, scraping up any browned bits stuck to the bottom. Let the wine simmer for 2-3 minutes to reduce slightly.
  5. Add the beef broth and Worcestershire sauce, stirring everything together.
  6. Return the seared beef (along with any juices) to the pot. Add the chopped carrots and potatoes, bay leaves, thyme, and rosemary.
  7. Bring the stew to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it cook gently for 1.5 to 2 hours, until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded.
  8. If the stew needs thickening, mix 1 tablespoon of flour with a bit of cold water to form a slurry, then stir it into the stew. Let it simmer for another 10 minutes to thicken.
  9. In the last 10 minutes of cooking, stir in the frozen peas (if using). Remove the bay leaves and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.
  10. Ladle the beef stew into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley if desired. Serve with crusty bread or over mashed potatoes.
